# QMS Video Script (10–15 minutes)

## Opening
Hello, in this video I will explain the QMS module in a Life Sciences Supply Chain OS. I will cover deviation management, CAPA, product complaints, recall management, adverse event reporting, and supplier management, along with API and raw material examples.

## What QMS means
QMS stands for Quality Management System. In life sciences, it ensures products and materials are controlled, traceable, and compliant so patient safety and product quality are protected.

## In-Process Quality
### Deviation Management
Explain that a deviation is any departure from SOP, batch record, or approved process.
Use API example: reactor temperature excursion.
Use raw material example: material used before final QA release.

### CAPA
Explain the difference between corrective and preventive action.
Use API example: wrong solvent charging sequence.
Use raw material example: moisture issue in excipient drums.

## In-Product Quality
### Product Complaints
Explain complaint intake, classification, investigation, and closure.
Use examples from API drum discoloration and packaging print mismatch.

### Recall Management
Explain why a recall is triggered and how stock tracing and reconciliation happen.

### Adverse Event Reporting
Explain that patient safety events must go to the safety/pharmacovigilance team quickly and still remain linked to quality records when relevant.

## QMS Management
### Supplier Management
Explain onboarding, qualification, approved list, monitoring, and requalification.
Use one API supplier and one excipient/raw material supplier example.

## Role-wise view
### QA Officer
Owns documentation, investigation follow-up, CAPA tracking, complaint review, compliance, and closure quality.

### Production Manager
Supports containment, gives process details, implements corrective actions on the floor, and balances output with discipline.

## Close
End by saying a good QMS module is traceable, evidence-based, workflow-driven, and important because it prevents both process failures and market quality risks.
